Why the Corn Snake Wins

The Corn Snake is often cited as the perfect starting point for new keepers. They are natively found across the southeastern United States, making them highly adaptable to captive conditions. They reach a manageable adult size (usually 3 to 5 feet) and possess a remarkably docile temperament, meaning they rarely bite and are excellent candidates for handling.

**Simple Feeding:** They feed on readily available frozen/thawed mice, only requiring a meal every 7 to 10 days, which significantly simplifies weekly care compared to daily feeding lizards.

**Longevity and Hardiness:** Corn Snakes are robust, forgiving of minor care mistakes, and can live for 15 to 20 years with proper attention, offering a long and rewarding experience for the devoted keeper.
